Introduction

Understanding patient rights and ethicals principles guiding health care is crucial for ensuring that patients receives proper care and respect. This webpage highlights key aspects of patient rights and medical ethics in healthcare.

Patient Rights are the subset of Human Rights.Minimum enforceable standards for the ways persons can expect to be treated by others are human rights. Customary standards for the ways persons in the given society are expected to treat others are Ethics. As such, every patient rights is derived from one or more medical or social ethical principles.

The concept of Health Care quality contains two primary factors as follows:

  • Providing a Service that meets the expectations of the primary customer/stakeholder(the patient) in the service transaction.
  • Standardizing the service so that the same level of services is provided across patients.

    • "Rights aren't rights if someone can take them away.They're Privileges."

Patient Rights

  • Right to Informed Consent:Patient have the rights to receive all necessary information regarding their condition and treatment options to make informed decisions.
  • Right to Privacy:Every patient is entitled to confidentiality regarding their personal health information.
  • Right to Access Medical Records:Patient can review their medical records at any time and request copies for themselves or another healthcare provider.
  • Right to Quality Care:Patients deserve safe, effective, and compassionate care from healthcare provider.
  • Right to Refuse Treatment: Patients have the autonomy to refuse medical treatments, even if it is against medical advice.

Ethicals Principles in Healthcare

  1. Autonomy:Healthcare providers must respect a patient's decision-making rights.
  2. Beneficence: Acting in the best interest of the patient and promoting their well-being.
  3. Non-maleficence: The obligation to not inflict harm intentionally.
  4. Justice: Ensuring fairness in medical decision-making and treatment distribution.
  5. Confidentiality:Respecting patient privacy and keeping health information secure.

Conclusion

Patient rights and medical ethics form the foundation of healthcare practice.By understanding these principles,both healthcare provider and patient can work towards a more respectful,fair,and efficient healthcare system.

As with all healthcare ethics dissussed,reaching morally acceptable desision and enforcing that decision as rights involves examining how the ethical principles interplay to determine which should take precedence.

Adhering to ethics in healthcare supports improved patients outcomes, increased trust and confidence in the healthcare system, and better overall health and well-being for individuals and communities.
