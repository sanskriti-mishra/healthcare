What Are the Different Types of Doctors?

You may call them simply doctors. But most doctors have extra expertise in one type of medicine or another. In fact, there are several hundred medical specialties and subspecialties. Here are the most common types of doctors you'll likely see.

  1. Cardiologists

    2. They're experts on the heart and blood vessels. You might see them for heart failure, a heart attack, high blood pressure, or an irregular heartbeat.

  2. Dermatologists

    3. Have problems with your skin, hair, nails? Do you have moles, scars, acne, or skin allergies? Dermatologists can help.

  3. Gastroenterologists

    4. They're specialists in digestive organs, including the stomach, bowels, pancreas, liver, and gallbladder. You might see them for abdominal pain, ulcers, diarrhea, jaundice, or cancers in your digestive organs. They also do a colonoscopy and other tests for colon cancer.

  4. Neurologists

    5. These are specialists in the nervous system, which includes the brain, spinal cord, and nerves. They treat strokes, brain and spinal tumors, epilepsy, Parkinson's disease, and Alzheimer's disease.

  5. Oncologists

    6. These internists are cancer specialists. They do chemotherapy treatments and often work with radiation oncologists and surgeons to care for someone with cancer.

  6. Ophthalmologists

    7. You call them eye doctors. They can prescribe glasses or contact lenses and diagnose and treat diseases like glaucoma. Unlike optometrists, they're medical doctors who can treat every kind of eye condition as well as operate on the eyes.

  7. Pathologists

    8. These lab doctors identify the causes of diseases by examining body tissues and fluids under microscopes.

  8. Pediatricians

    9. They care for children from birth to young adulthood. Some pediatricians specialize in pre-teens and teens, child abuse, or children's developmental issues.

  9. Physiatrists

    10. These specialists in physical medicine and rehabilitation treat neck or back pain and sports or spinal cord injuries as well as other disabilities caused by accidents or diseases.

  10. Orthopedic

    11. An orthopedic doctor (also called an orthopedist) treats injuries and diseases involving muscles, bones, joints, ligaments, and tendons.

  11. Physiotherapist

    12. Physiotherapists can also specialize in different areas, including:

    • Sports medicine

      • Physiotherapists who specialize in sports medicine help to alleviate and heal injuries caused while playing sports.

    • Orthopedic physiotherapy

      • Physiotherapists who specialize in orthopedic physiotherapy focus on treating musculoskeletal conditions and injuries, such as arthritis, back pain, sprains, and strains.

  12. Gynaecologist

    13. A gynaecologist is a specialist doctor who manages conditions that affect the female reproductive system — which includes the vulva, vagina, uterus (womb) and ovaries.

  13. Dentist

    14. A dentist is a doctor who diagnoses and treats oral health conditions.
